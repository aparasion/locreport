A recent article highlights the critical distinction between using a static glossary and a dynamic term base in localization workflows. While glossaries can suffice for simple, low-volume projects managed by a single team, they fall short in scenarios involving multiple languages and contributors, where consistency becomes an operational necessity. A term base not only anchors terminology to specific concepts but also ensures alignment across various teams—marketing, product, and support—preventing brand inconsistency.

The article emphasizes that as AI-generated content proliferates, the risk of terminology errors increases significantly. Without a structured term base, inconsistencies can quickly compound across languages, leading to confusion and potential brand dilution. A modern term base integrates seamlessly into existing workflows, providing context-rich, enforceable terminology that guides both human translators and AI tools.

For localization professionals, the key takeaway is the importance of implementing a term base to maintain quality and consistency, especially as AI plays a larger role in content creation. This proactive approach can save time and reduce costly rework, ensuring that all output aligns with brand standards.
