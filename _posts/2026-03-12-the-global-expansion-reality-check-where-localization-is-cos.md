Global expansion is set to accelerate in 2026, but a recent survey by Lokalise reveals a troubling gap between companies' ambitions and their localization capabilities. Among 500 global business leaders, 36% plan to enter two or more new markets, yet many express concerns about translation quality and budget constraints. Poor localization is costing businesses an estimated 20% of potential revenue annually, highlighting the critical need for effective localization strategies that align with growth plans.

As companies eye high-growth regions like Latin America and Southeast Asia, they must address the underinvestment in localization that often accompanies market expansion. While 63% of respondents are using AI-powered translation tools, over half still worry about accuracy and cultural nuances. This disconnect suggests that while the tools are available, the strategic implementation of localization remains a challenge.

For localization professionals, the key takeaway is clear: integrating localization into the core of growth strategies is essential. Companies that prioritize localization as a revenue driver rather than a cost will be better positioned to capitalize on new market opportunities and enhance their competitive edge.
