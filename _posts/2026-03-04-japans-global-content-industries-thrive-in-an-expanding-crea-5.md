Stanford University's Shorenstein Asia-Pacific Research Center (APARC) is a leading institution focused on interdisciplinary research and policy dialogue regarding contemporary Asia. It aims to address critical challenges in the region while educating future experts in Asian affairs. APARC engages in various activities, including producing policy-relevant research and fostering public understanding of Asian issues.

Recent publications from APARC scholars include works on America's trade policy in the Asia-Pacific, China's rise as a global power, and the dynamics between the U.S. and Southeast Asia. Notably, former diplomat Rahm Emanuel participated in an APARC briefing discussing the current state of American politics and its implications for U.S. alliances in the Indo-Pacific, drawing lessons from Japan's experiences.

This center plays a vital role in bridging research and practice, influencing decision-makers in both the U.S. and Asia. For more details on their initiatives and findings, readers are encouraged to explore the full article.
