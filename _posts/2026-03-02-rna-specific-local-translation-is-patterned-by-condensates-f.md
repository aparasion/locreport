The article discusses a study published in *Nature* that reveals how RNA-specific local translation is organized by cellular condensates to facilitate growth in multinucleate cells. Researchers found that these condensates play a crucial role in spatially regulating the translation of mRNA, which is essential for the proper functioning and development of multinucleate cells, such as muscle fibers. The findings suggest that the formation of these condensates is vital for maintaining cellular organization and efficient protein synthesis. This research has significant implications for understanding muscle development and regeneration, as well as potential applications in treating muscle-related diseases. The study highlights the intricate relationship between RNA localization, translation regulation, and cellular architecture in complex tissues.
