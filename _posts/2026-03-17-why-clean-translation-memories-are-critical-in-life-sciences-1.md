Vistatec emphasizes the critical role of clean and well-maintained translation memories (TMs) in the life sciences localization process. TMs, which store previously translated text for reuse, can significantly enhance efficiency and consistency. However, the article warns that poorly managed TMs can lead to compliance risks, such as mixing patient-facing language with regulatory content, which can delay approvals and damage brand reputation.

The localization and language services industry must recognize the importance of maintaining distinct TMs tailored to specific audiences and purposes. By implementing rigorous quality checks and tagging systems, LSPs can mitigate the risks associated with contaminated TMs. This structured approach not only supports compliance but also fosters trust in patient communications, which is paramount in life sciences.

The key takeaway for localization professionals is to prioritize the cleanliness and organization of TMs. Regular audits and strategic planning are essential to ensure that translation assets align with the specific requirements of each project, ultimately safeguarding both compliance and communication integrity.
