At SlatorCon Remote March 2026, industry leaders from Ajax Systems and Brave discussed the unique challenges of localization in privacy-focused sectors. The panel, moderated by Slator’s Alex Edwards, highlighted the complexities of involving multiple internal stakeholders in the localization process, as noted by Ajax's Olena Azanova. She emphasized that while not every localization step requires approval from all teams, the sheer number of contributors complicates workflows significantly.

This discussion underscores a critical trend in the localization industry: the increasing necessity for privacy-first companies to navigate diverse regulations and cultural nuances. Both Azanova and Brave's Andy Andersen pointed out the limitations of AI translation in these contexts, stressing the need for human oversight to ensure accuracy and brand voice, especially when dealing with sensitive content. They also noted that while AI can accelerate certain tasks, it has not yet reached a level where it can fully replace human input.

A key takeaway for localization professionals is the importance of integrating human-led processes with AI tools in a way that maintains quality and compliance. As privacy regulations evolve, understanding the balance between automation and human oversight will be crucial for successful localization strategies in privacy-sensitive markets.
