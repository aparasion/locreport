The SlatorCon Remote event on March 24, 2026, brought together over 400 language professionals, featuring insights from industry leaders like Esther Bond and Florian Faes. The event highlighted significant market developments, including ElevenLabs’ impressive USD 11 billion valuation and the growing influence of AI in translation and dubbing. Faes emphasized the emergence of Language Solutions Integrators (LSIs) and Language Technology Platforms (LTPs) as key players in the evolving landscape, while Anna Wyndham discussed the critical role of data-for-AI, predicting its market growth from USD 9 billion to USD 21 billion.

This surge in activity reflects broader trends in the localization industry, where AI-driven workflows and expert oversight are reshaping traditional practices. The discussions underscored the necessity for localization professionals to adapt to rapid technological advancements while addressing privacy and quality standards in their services. 

A key takeaway for localization experts is the importance of aligning automation with regulatory demands, as well as the need to focus on the qualitative aspects of localization, ensuring that customer needs are met through effective communication strategies.
