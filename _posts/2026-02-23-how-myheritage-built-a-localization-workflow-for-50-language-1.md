MyHeritage, a leader in AI and genetic health, has reached a significant milestone by localizing its platform into 50 languages, driven by the vision of Valentina Wainstein, who emphasizes the importance of breaking down language barriers in genealogy. The company, now valued at around $1 billion, has centralized its localization workflow using Crowdin Enterprise, allowing it to efficiently manage millions of words across its global ecosystem.

The integration of advanced AI tools, including DeepL and GPT-4o mini, alongside a dedicated community of volunteers, has enabled MyHeritage to balance high-tech automation with human nuance. This sophisticated approach ensures that translations maintain cultural accuracy, particularly in complex genealogy terminology, which can vary significantly across languages.

As MyHeritage continues to expand, localization professionals can glean insights on the importance of centralizing workflows and leveraging both technology and community expertise to enhance translation quality. This case highlights the need for careful planning and collaboration in managing large-scale localization projects.
