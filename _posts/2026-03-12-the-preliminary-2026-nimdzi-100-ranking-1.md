The 2026 Nimdzi 100 ranking is set to include both language service providers (LSPs) and technology solution companies, reflecting the industry's evolution amid rapid advancements in artificial intelligence. This update comes as Nimdzi compiles its annual list of the largest language providers globally, based on self-reported revenues and extensive research. The ranking indicates a significant shift, with traditional translation and localization services accounting for 68.4% of total revenue, while interpreting services and technology offerings follow at 19.3% and 12.3%, respectively.

Despite a slight dip in overall optimism—averaging 6.2 for the industry compared to 6.5 last year—providers remain hopeful about their individual business prospects, rating them at 7.0. This sentiment suggests resilience in the face of market changes, although the data may be skewed by non-responses from struggling companies.

Localization professionals should prepare for the full Nimdzi 100 report, which promises deeper insights into industry dynamics and revenue trends, set to release at the end of March.
