This article explores the transformative impact of computational methods on language documentation and description, particularly for languages with limited digital resources. The authors survey the historical context of these methods and introduce advanced techniques, such as large language models (LLMs), that enhance various aspects of linguistic research, including data collection, transcription, and morphosyntactic analysis. 

Key findings indicate that these computational approaches not only streamline traditional methodologies but also raise ethical considerations regarding the involvement of speech communities in the documentation process. The integration of data mining and machine learning necessitates a reevaluation of best practices in linguistic fieldwork, emphasizing the importance of community engagement in language preservation efforts.

The study underscores the theoretical significance of blending computational linguistics with documentary practices, suggesting that as LLMs and user-friendly computational tools become more prevalent, they will play a crucial role in shaping future linguistic research and ethical frameworks in language technology.
