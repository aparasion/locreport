Recent advancements in artificial intelligence are transforming language documentation and description methodologies, particularly for languages with limited digital resources. This article examines the evolution of computational techniques, including the application of large language models (LLMs), which are poised to enhance various aspects of linguistic research, such as data collection, transcription, and translation.

The implications for the localization and language services industry are significant. As these computational methods gain traction, they will influence how linguists and language professionals approach fieldwork, necessitating a reevaluation of ethical best practices. The integration of machine learning and data mining can streamline workflows, but it also raises questions about community involvement in the documentation and preservation of their languages.

One key takeaway for localization professionals is the importance of adapting to these emerging technologies while ensuring ethical engagement with language communities. As LLMs and user-friendly computational tools become more prevalent, they will shape the future of language documentation and localization strategies.
