In 2026, effective software localization teams are no longer treating localization as a post-development task; instead, they are integrating it into the development process. By utilizing dynamic visual context and structuring in-country reviews as repeatable workflows, these teams can run localization in parallel with engineering, significantly improving efficiency and reducing bottlenecks.

This shift is crucial for the localization and language services industry, as traditional sequential workflows struggle to keep pace with rapid development cycles. The article highlights how teams using advanced translation management systems (TMS) and dynamic context tools, like Rigi, have reported substantial decreases in developer support time and faster turnaround for translations. The integration of real-time visual context allows translators to see how strings fit into the UI, minimizing context-related questions and enhancing overall quality.

For localization professionals, the key takeaway is the importance of adopting a parallel workflow model to streamline processes and improve product quality. I highly recommend exploring the full article for deeper insights into these transformative strategies.
