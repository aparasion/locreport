At CES 2025, Samsung unveiled its Live Translate feature for TVs, expanding a capability previously available on its phones and tablets. This real-time translation tool will initially support seven languages by translating closed captions during live broadcasts, enhancing accessibility for diverse audiences. The feature builds on the success of Live Translate introduced with the Galaxy S24, which supports 13 languages.

This development highlights a growing trend in the localization and language services industry, where real-time translation and accessibility features are becoming essential in consumer electronics. By integrating AI-driven solutions like Voice Removal with Audio Subtitles, Samsung is addressing the needs of visually impaired users, indicating a shift towards more inclusive technology that caters to a broader audience.

Localization professionals should consider the implications of such advancements, as they may influence content creation and adaptation strategies for multimedia platforms.
