Meta is enhancing its AI-powered translation capabilities for Instagram and Facebook by adding support for Hindi and Portuguese, targeting creators in India and Brazil. This move follows the initial launch of the feature in August, which included English and Spanish. Instagram head Adam Mosseri emphasized the importance of breaking down language barriers to help creators reach wider audiences and grow their followings.

For localization and language services professionals, this development underscores a growing trend toward integrating AI-driven translation in social media platforms, which can significantly impact content accessibility and audience engagement. The ability for users to enable automatic translation and for creators to select translation options before publishing their Reels signals a shift in how content can be tailored for diverse linguistic markets. 

As Meta continues to innovate with features like voice dubbing and text translation, localization professionals should consider how these advancements may influence content strategies and the overall user experience in multilingual environments.
