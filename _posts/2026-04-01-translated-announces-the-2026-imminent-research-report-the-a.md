Translated has announced the upcoming release of its 2026 Research Report, "The Age of Experience in AI," which highlights a transformative shift in artificial intelligence. This report, produced by Translated's research center Imminent, explores the evolution from AI systems reliant on static historical data to those capable of learning through real-time interaction with their environments. CEO Marco Trombetti emphasizes that this marks a pivotal moment where AI can not only process information but also develop a form of perception.

This development is significant for the localization and language services industry, as it suggests a future where AI can adapt to diverse cultural contexts and user needs. The report discusses the importance of pluralistic AI models that reflect the complexities of language and culture, moving away from a one-size-fits-all approach. As AI systems become more context-aware, they could enhance localization workflows, enabling more nuanced and culturally relevant translations.

A key takeaway for localization professionals is the potential for "Experience in AI" to reshape how we approach translation and localization projects. By leveraging AI that understands cultural nuances, companies can create more effective and tailored communication strategies in global markets.
