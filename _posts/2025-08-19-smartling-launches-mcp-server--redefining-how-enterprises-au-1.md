Smartling has launched its Model Context Protocol (MCP) server, enabling seamless integration of its translation capabilities into various AI platforms like Claude Code and Visual Studio Code. This new standard, developed by Anthropic, allows software engineers and content teams to access high-quality translations directly within their existing workflows, eliminating the need for switching applications or managing multiple logins.

The MCP server is significant for the localization and language services industry as it enhances translation efficiency by leveraging customers’ translation memories, glossaries, and style guides. By integrating Smartling's translation technology with preferred neural machine translation engines or large language models, businesses can achieve faster, more accurate translations while maintaining brand consistency. This development reflects a growing trend toward embedding translation capabilities into AI-driven workflows, which is crucial for companies looking to streamline their localization processes.

A key takeaway for localization professionals is that the Smartling MCP server represents a shift towards more integrated and context-aware translation solutions, making it easier to maintain quality and consistency across diverse content channels and markets.
