Stand for Children, a nonprofit focused on equitable education, recently partnered with Preferred Curriculum Solutions to deliver an extensive translation project for their Great First Eight Curriculum. This initiative aimed to expand the Pre-Kindergarten curriculum into a full-day Dual-Language format, translating over 600,000 words into Spanish while adhering to specific educational standards and aggressive deadlines.

This project highlights the increasing demand for localized educational resources, particularly in dual-language settings. The collaboration involved a diverse team, including project managers, linguists, and quality assessors, all working within the constraints of the Marq publishing platform. Unique challenges arose due to non-editable files and the lack of translation memory reuse, necessitating a bespoke workflow that maximized efficiency and maintained quality throughout the process.

For localization professionals, this case underscores the importance of adaptable workflows and comprehensive glossaries in educational projects.
