B2B SaaS companies often approach localization with a simplistic "send content in, get translations out" mindset, which can lead to chaos as they scale. In a recent episode of The Agile Localization Podcast, Nicola Calabrese, founder of Undertow, emphasizes the need for structured, AI-powered localization programs. He outlines four essential pillars for successful localization: strategy aligned with business goals, skilled personnel to navigate complex workflows, modern technology infrastructure, and clear processes to eliminate bottlenecks.

This discussion highlights a critical shift in the localization landscape, where AI is not a panacea but an amplifier of existing challenges. Calabrese warns that without a centralized translation management system (TMS) and defined workflows, companies risk operational breakdown as they expand into new markets. He advocates for a Minimum Viable Experience (MVE) approach, focusing on essential customer journey touchpoints to gather actionable data.

For localization professionals, the key takeaway is the importance of integrating strategy, skilled resources, and technology to create scalable localization efforts. Embracing a structured approach not only enhances efficiency but also positions localization as a growth enabler rather than a cost center.
