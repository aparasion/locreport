The recent passage of the Supporting Patient Education and Knowledge (SPEAK) Act marks a significant advancement in language access within telehealth, requiring the Department of Health and Human Services (HHS) to establish standardized practices for integrating interpreters and multilingual resources into virtual healthcare. This legislation aims to bridge the gap for the 25 million Americans with limited English proficiency, who face barriers to accessing essential healthcare services.

The SPEAK Act's swift implementation reflects a growing recognition of the importance of language access in healthcare, especially as telehealth becomes increasingly prevalent. By mandating best practices for communication, the act addresses critical issues such as medication errors and longer recovery times that arise from language barriers, ultimately aiming to improve health outcomes and reduce costs for all stakeholders.

For localization professionals, the ongoing developments surrounding the Language Access for All Act and the SPEAK Act present an opportunity to engage in shaping best practices and standards.
