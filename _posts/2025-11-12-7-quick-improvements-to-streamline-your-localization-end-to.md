Localization leaders are facing increasing demands to optimize their processes without significant investment in new technologies. A recent article by Roman Civin outlines seven practical improvements that can lead to immediate benefits in localization operations. These strategies, derived from real client projects, emphasize incremental changes that enhance efficiency while maintaining quality.

The suggested improvements include streamlining localization request intake, implementing content tiering, and adopting a minimum viable “MT-first” approach. By focusing on these areas, organizations can reduce manual errors, speed up workflows, and better align localization efforts with business objectives. For instance, automating request processes can significantly cut down on redundant communications and improve turnaround times.

A key takeaway for localization professionals is the importance of defining and measuring relevant KPIs that resonate with stakeholders, such as time-to-market or user engagement. These early wins can build confidence in localization capabilities and demonstrate its value as a growth enabler.
