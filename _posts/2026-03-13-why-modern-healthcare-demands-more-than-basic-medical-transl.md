The 2026 CCHI Power of Practice II Conference underscored the critical need for specialized medical interpretation in oncology, emphasizing that general medical proficiency is no longer sufficient. Complex terminology such as "fractionation" can significantly impact patient safety and informed consent, making specialized training essential for interpreters in high-stakes clinical environments.

The conference also addressed the ethical complexities interpreters face, advocating for structured decision-making frameworks that prioritize patient outcomes over mere compliance. This approach encourages interpreters to navigate conflicts between accuracy, neutrality, and confidentiality, ensuring that ethical considerations directly inform their practice.

One key takeaway is that sight translation requires distinct competencies and should not be treated as a routine extension of general interpreting. Organizations must empower interpreters to decline out-of-scope requests to protect all parties involved.
