Smartling has reported a staggering 218% growth in its AI and AI-human translation services in 2025, signaling a pivotal moment in the localization industry. This surge is attributed to enterprises transitioning from experimental AI applications to full-scale production deployments. As organizations increasingly recognize the potential of AI to enhance translation workflows, Smartling is positioning itself as a leader in this rapidly evolving landscape, making its advancements worthy of close attention from localization managers, language technology leaders, and enterprise buyers.

This development aligns with a broader trend in the localization industry where AI technologies are moving from pilot projects to integral components of translation strategies. The growing demand for faster, more efficient translation solutions is reshaping how businesses operate. Companies are no longer satisfied with basic translation services; they require sophisticated tools that can seamlessly integrate AI into their existing workflows. As enterprises grapple with the complexities of global communication, the need for scalable solutions that maintain quality while reducing costs has never been more pronounced. Smartling’s enhancements, such as the AI Hub and automatic hallucination detection, reflect this shift towards comprehensive AI integration.

The impact of Smartling’s advancements on localization workflows is significant. By providing tools that allow customers to select their AI providers and enhance translation quality through AI post-editing, Smartling is empowering localization teams to optimize their processes. The introduction of features like the LQA Agent, which streamlines quality evaluations, directly addresses the challenges faced by human reviewers, reducing their workload while maintaining high standards. This shift not only enhances the efficiency of localization teams but also alters competitive dynamics within the industry, as companies that adopt these innovations can achieve faster turnaround times and lower costs, giving them an edge in the global market.

Looking ahead, Smartling’s growth trajectory signals a critical evolution in the localization industry: the increasing reliance on AI as a foundational element of translation strategies. As enterprises continue to embrace AI-driven solutions, the demand for platforms that offer robust integration, quality assurance, and scalability will intensify. Localization managers and language technology leaders must adapt to this changing landscape, recognizing that the future of translation will be defined by those who can effectively harness AI capabilities. This trend underscores the importance of investing in advanced technology to remain competitive in an increasingly globalized economy.
