Video localisation is gaining traction as a critical component for global content strategies, moving beyond simple translation to create culturally resonant experiences. As businesses increasingly leverage video—91% now use it as a marketing tool—localisation becomes essential to engage diverse audiences. A poorly localised video can lead to significant drops in viewer engagement and trust, highlighting the need for comprehensive adaptation that includes language, cultural references, and visual elements.

The rise of AI-driven tools has transformed video localisation, making it more accessible and cost-effective. Companies can now produce localised content faster than ever, with methods like source-level creation enabling simultaneous production of multiple language versions. This shift allows teams to tailor their content more effectively, ensuring that it resonates with local audiences while maintaining brand consistency.

For localisation professionals, the key takeaway is to prioritize video localisation in your workflows. By adopting best practices—such as designing for localisation from the outset and implementing structured QA processes—you can enhance viewer engagement and drive better business outcomes across global markets.
