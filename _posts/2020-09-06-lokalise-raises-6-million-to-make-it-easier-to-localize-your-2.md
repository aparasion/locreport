Lokalise, a Latvian startup specializing in the translation and localization of digital content, has secured $6 million in funding to enhance its software-as-a-service platform. This investment, led by Mike Chalfen and supported by notable investors, aims to streamline the often cumbersome process of updating multilingual text across apps, websites, and games. Many companies still rely on outdated tools like Excel for localization, leading to inefficiencies during product updates.

The Lokalise platform addresses these challenges by allowing teams to integrate directly with GitHub or GitLab, facilitating automatic updates of language files. Its collaborative features enable team members to communicate effectively, assign tasks, and track progress, significantly improving workflow. With 1,500 customers, including major brands like Revolut and Yelp, Lokalise is poised to capitalize on the growing demand for efficient localization solutions.

For localization professionals, Lokalise represents a shift towards more integrated and automated workflows, emphasizing the need for modern tools that can keep pace with rapid development cycles in a global marketplace.
