Phrase has been actively hosting its Loc360° events across major global cities, including London, Berlin, and Singapore, bringing together localization managers, content strategists, and AI specialists. These gatherings have revealed a significant shift in how the localization industry perceives itself, transitioning from viewing translation as a cost center to recognizing it as a vital growth function. This evolution emphasizes the need for localization teams to connect their work to business outcomes, moving beyond traditional metrics like word counts to metrics that reflect market expansion and revenue generation.

The discussions highlighted a common challenge: localization teams must claim their strategic role within organizations. This involves redefining their value in terms that resonate with business leaders, focusing on revenue impact rather than operational efficiency. AI is also reshaping the localization landscape, enabling teams to shift from reactive execution to proactive insights, though challenges around quality and trust remain.

For localization professionals, the key takeaway is clear: to secure a seat at the strategic table, teams must align their efforts with broader business goals and demonstrate measurable impact. This approach not only enhances their relevance but also fosters deeper collaboration across departments.
