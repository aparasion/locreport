Jordan is emerging as a notable player in the Middle East's tech landscape, highlighted by the inclusion of Dakwak, a website translation and localization platform, in the latest 500 Startups cohort. Founded by Waheed Barghouthi, Dakwak aims to simplify the global expansion process for small- to medium-sized businesses by offering easy-to-use translation tools. The platform, launched just six weeks ago, allows users to translate their websites by simply entering a URL and selecting a target language, with options for both machine and professional translations.

This development is significant for the localization industry as it addresses a common misconception that English is the primary language for global business. Dakwak's approach not only enhances the availability of Arabic content online but also supports a wider range of languages, catering to a diverse client base. The platform's user-friendly dashboard streamlines the translation process, reducing operational headaches for website owners.

For localization professionals, Dakwak represents a shift towards more accessible and efficient translation solutions, emphasizing the importance of adapting to a multilingual world. As the company seeks to expand its reach in the U.S., it highlights the growing demand for localization services that can keep pace with global digital content needs.
