Justine Bastos, CEO of Loop Translations, emphasizes a people-first approach in the localization industry, focusing on project management expertise and transparency. With a team experienced in LSP operations, Loop prioritizes understanding client needs and maintaining open communication. The company also stands out for its commitment to social responsibility, donating a portion of profits to local causes and offering pro-bono services for NGOs and sensitive cases.

This approach not only enhances client relationships but also aligns with emerging trends in the localization sector, where companies are expected to act as integrators and advisors rather than mere service providers. Bastos highlights the importance of adapting to the evolving landscape, including supporting AI-driven language technologies.

For localization professionals, the key takeaway is the importance of building a company culture that prioritizes client engagement and social impact, positioning LSPs like Loop as leaders in a rapidly changing market.
