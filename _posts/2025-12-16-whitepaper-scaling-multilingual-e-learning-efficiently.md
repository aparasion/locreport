A new whitepaper by Laszlo K. Varga and Beata Janaszkiewicz-Cavalcanti highlights the explosive growth of the e-learning industry, now valued between USD 250-300 billion, which significantly outpaces the language services sector. This surge, driven by the demand for global reach through advanced technology platforms, underscores the critical need for localized content to enhance audience engagement and knowledge retention.

For localization professionals, the findings emphasize the increasing reliance on AI-driven localization solutions. Traditional human-only workflows struggle to meet the scale required for effective multilingual e-learning, making AI tools not just a preference but a necessity for large-scale projects. This shift not only addresses cost-effectiveness but also enables the creation of courses in learners' native languages, fostering a more inclusive learning environment.
