XTM International has made a significant move in the localization industry by acquiring Consoltec, the developer of the FlowFit Translation Business Management System (TBMS). This acquisition, announced on October 8, 2025, aims to enhance XTM's capabilities in enterprise localization operations, combining the strengths of two leading solutions: XTRF and FlowFit. The integration of these technologies is positioned to address the increasing demands for faster, high-quality multilingual content delivery while maintaining stringent security standards, a critical concern for enterprises today.

This development is part of a broader trend in the localization sector where companies are increasingly seeking integrated solutions that streamline operations and enhance efficiency. As globalization accelerates, enterprises face mounting pressure to produce and manage multilingual content at scale. The localization industry is witnessing a shift towards AI-driven technologies that not only automate processes but also improve quality control and compliance. The acquisition of Consoltec aligns with this trend, as it combines XTM's AI capabilities with FlowFit's robust project management features, creating a more comprehensive platform for enterprise localization.

The impact of this acquisition is multifaceted, particularly for localization managers and teams within enterprises. The integration of FlowFit into XTM's existing suite provides a centralized platform that simplifies project management, resource allocation, and financial oversight. Localization teams can expect enhanced visibility into their operations, allowing for better decision-making and increased efficiency. Furthermore, the focus on security and compliance within FlowFit addresses the needs of regulated industries—such as finance and government—where data protection is paramount. Language service providers (LSPs) will also benefit from the combined offerings, as they can leverage the advanced capabilities of both systems to better serve their enterprise clients.

Looking ahead, this acquisition signals a pivotal shift towards more integrated and secure localization solutions in the industry. As enterprises continue to navigate the complexities of global markets, the demand for comprehensive platforms that can manage every aspect of localization will only grow. XTM's strategic move to enhance its TBMS offerings reflects an understanding of this need, positioning the company as a key player in the evolving landscape of enterprise localization. The LocReport editorial team observes that as companies increasingly prioritize security, scalability, and efficiency, we can expect further consolidation and innovation within the localization technology space, reshaping how organizations approach multilingual content management.
