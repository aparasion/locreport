Zurich University of Applied Sciences (ZHAW) has appointed Alice Delorme Benites, PhD, as the new Head of the Department of Applied Linguistics, effective November 1, 2026. Delorme Benites, who has been with ZHAW since 2013, brings a wealth of experience from her roles in teaching and administration, as well as a background in freelance translation. Her focus will be on integrating AI technologies into the curriculum while emphasizing the importance of human expertise in language and communication.

This leadership change is significant for the localization and language services industry as it signals a commitment to blending AI with human skills in education. Delorme Benites aims to prepare students for the evolving landscape of language technology, ensuring they understand both the practical applications and the broader implications of AI on global communication. Her interdisciplinary approach will enhance collaboration with industry partners, addressing real-world challenges in multilingual communication.

A key takeaway for localization professionals is Delorme Benites' vision of fostering accountability in AI-driven processes. As AI becomes more prevalent, the need for skilled professionals who can navigate the complexities of technology and human communication will be paramount, highlighting the importance of education that bridges theory and practice.
