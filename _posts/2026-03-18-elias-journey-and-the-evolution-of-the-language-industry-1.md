ELIA recently celebrated its 20th anniversary, reflecting on its evolution alongside the European language industry. In a special episode of Localization Today, key figures from ELIA’s leadership, including co-founder Roberto Ganzerli and current president Anu Carnegie-Brown, discussed the association's origins, its phases of growth, and the ongoing importance of collaboration in navigating industry changes. 

This milestone highlights the critical role ELIA has played in fostering a pan-European space for language service companies to share knowledge and adapt to market dynamics. As the industry faces challenges from AI and shifting client expectations, ELIA has transformed from a networking platform into a vital resource for strategic sense-making and professional development. The conversations emphasize that the association's strength lies in its community, where leaders can openly exchange ideas and support each other through uncertainty.

For localization professionals, the key takeaway is the importance of peer collaboration in adapting to the rapidly changing landscape. Engaging with industry associations like ELIA can provide valuable insights and foster resilience in the face of disruption.
