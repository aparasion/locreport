Adam Asnes, CEO of Lingoport, recently discussed the evolving landscape of localization in software development during his appearance on VistaTalks. He emphasized the necessity for localization to transition from a one-off task to a continuous, integrated process within development workflows. Lingoport’s journey began with recognizing the inefficiencies in traditional localization methods, which often resulted in repeated fixes due to software updates causing drift. This insight led to the creation of their product, Globalyzer, and a broader vision for embedding localization from the design phase onward.

As software teams adopt faster release cycles, the need for a seamless localization process has never been more critical. Lingoport Localizer enables localization to occur alongside design and development, allowing teams to address issues in real-time. This integration not only enhances quality but also aligns localization with agile methodologies, transforming it into a vital component of the software creation process.

The key takeaway for localization professionals is the importance of embedding localization early in the development lifecycle. By doing so, teams can improve efficiency, reduce bottlenecks, and ultimately leverage localization as a strategic advantage in delivering high-quality global products.
