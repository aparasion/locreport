Global expansion is set to accelerate in 2026, with companies planning to increase their market entries by 36%. However, a recent survey by Lokalise of 500 global business leaders reveals a troubling disconnect between ambition and execution in localization strategies. Many organizations are moving into new markets faster than their localization capabilities can support, leading to significant revenue losses—estimated at 20% annually due to poor localization practices. This gap between intention and execution is critical for localization managers, language technology leaders, and enterprise language buyers to understand, as it directly impacts their operational effectiveness and market success.

This development aligns with a broader trend in the localization industry where the importance of localization is increasingly recognized, yet execution often lags behind. As companies eye high-growth regions like Latin America and Southeast Asia, they face significant localization challenges that hinder their ability to capitalize on these opportunities. Despite 85% of leaders acknowledging the importance of localization, only 28% feel their efforts are "very strong." This disparity highlights a pervasive issue: while the potential for growth is clear, many organizations are not adequately investing in the localization resources necessary to support their expansion goals.

The implications for localization workflows and business models are profound. Teams responsible for localization must now navigate a landscape where speed to market is paramount, but budget constraints and quality concerns persist. For instance, 61% of companies cite cost as a major barrier to international expansion, and 36% have already delayed market entries due to localization challenges. As AI-powered translation becomes more mainstream—adopted by 63% of companies—there is a pressing need for localization teams to balance efficiency with quality. The reliance on AI, while beneficial for scaling, raises concerns about accuracy and cultural nuance, necessitating a hybrid approach that combines human oversight with technological solutions.

Ultimately, the findings from the Lokalise survey signal a pivotal moment for the localization industry. Companies that recognize localization as a core component of their growth strategy rather than a mere operational task will likely outperform their competitors. This means aligning localization budgets and efforts with actual market opportunities, particularly in under-invested regions. As the industry evolves, localization professionals must advocate for the resources and strategic focus necessary to bridge the gap between ambition and execution, ensuring that their organizations can effectively seize the opportunities presented by global expansion.
