At SlatorCon Remote, Bryan Forrester, CEO of Boostlingo, highlighted a critical issue in the interpreting industry: the majority of interpreting quality remains unmeasured and invisible. Forrester pointed out that the traditional Quality Assurance (QA) model relies heavily on spot-checking, which fails to provide comprehensive visibility into interpreting sessions. This lack of oversight not only undermines the quality of service but also affects client operations, as inconsistent interpreting can lead to miscommunication and operational inefficiencies. The introduction of AI-powered tools to enhance quality monitoring marks a pivotal moment for the industry, warranting the attention of localization managers, language technology leaders, and enterprise language buyers.

The shift from anecdotal evidence to data-driven quality assessment is part of a broader trend toward greater accountability and transparency in language services. As businesses increasingly rely on remote interpreting for critical communications, the demand for consistent quality has never been higher. Traditional methods of evaluating interpreting quality are becoming inadequate in a landscape where clients expect measurable outcomes. The advent of AI technologies that can analyze and score interpreting sessions in real-time signifies a response to this demand, moving the industry toward a more standardized approach to quality assurance.

The implications of this shift are profound for localization workflows and business models. Teams that previously relied on human reviewers for quality checks may need to adapt to integrating AI tools that provide objective scoring across multiple dimensions, such as contextual accuracy and professionalism. This change will likely lead to a reallocation of resources, where human interpreters are focused on high-stakes interactions, while AI handles routine assessments. Vendors that can leverage these AI capabilities will gain a competitive edge, as they can offer clients not only improved quality but also faster feedback loops and enhanced trust in the interpreting process.

For localization professionals, the evolution of interpreting quality assessment signals a critical juncture in the industry. As Forrester aptly noted, “quality is the unit of trust,” and the ability to provide measurable, consistent quality will be essential for client retention and satisfaction. The integration of AI into the interpreting workflow not only enhances quality assurance but also aligns with the increasing expectation for data security and compliance in language services. This trend suggests that the future of interpreting will be defined by a hybrid model where AI and human expertise coexist, driving a more reliable and efficient service delivery framework. As the industry continues to embrace these technological advancements, stakeholders must remain vigilant in refining their approaches to ensure that the human touch is not lost amid the data-driven revolution.
