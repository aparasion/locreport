XTM CEO Lorcan Malone emphasizes a pivotal shift in localization: treating it as an orchestrated supply chain rather than a series of isolated translation tasks. As global content management grows increasingly complex, successful localization teams are integrating diverse tools and systems to streamline workflows. Malone highlights that disconnected tools lead to inefficiencies, underscoring the need for a composable ecosystem where various solutions work seamlessly together.

This transformation is crucial as businesses face rising consumer expectations for multilingual content. Companies are now required to localize into more languages to meet these demands, and leveraging AI can enhance productivity without sacrificing quality. XTM’s Intelligent Post-Editing and XTM Agent exemplify this approach, allowing for automated quality assessments while keeping human oversight intact.

The key takeaway for localization professionals is to embrace this orchestrated mindset. By integrating tools and using intelligent automation, teams can enhance their efficiency and responsiveness, ultimately delivering high-quality localized content at scale in a rapidly evolving market.
