The article "What is Language Risk and Why Does it Matter?" emphasizes the critical importance of assessing and managing language risk within organizations as they increasingly rely on AI and automation for translation. The author argues that while advancements in technology have made translation more accessible, they come with significant caveats regarding accuracy and responsibility. Language risk is not a one-size-fits-all concern; it varies by content type, target audience, and regulatory environment, necessitating a nuanced approach to translation strategy.

To illustrate the complexities of language risk, the author recounts a past experience with a compliance manager who assessed translation requirements against regulatory frameworks for various countries. This historical perspective highlights the shift from a simplistic cost-benefit analysis—where translation decisions were primarily driven by legal compliance and potential penalties—to a more comprehensive evaluation that considers brand impact, customer satisfaction, and the quality of translation. The conversation with the compliance manager reveals how organizations previously weighed the costs of localization against the likelihood of enforcement, often prioritizing financial considerations over user experience. 

Today, the landscape of language risk has evolved. Organizations must now navigate a broader array of factors, including the variable quality of AI-generated translations, the implications of accessibility laws, and the expectations of customers who may refuse to engage with content they cannot read. The author stresses that language risk management is no longer limited to compliance; it encompasses a strategic understanding of how language affects communication with diverse audiences. This shift calls for a structured approach to language risk that aligns content types and regulatory requirements with the appropriate translation methods—whether AI, human, or hybrid.

For localization managers and language technology leaders, the article underscores the necessity of conducting thorough content audits and risk assessments. Understanding the language risk landscape allows organizations to make informed decisions about how to route content based on its risk profile. By doing so, they can optimize their translation workflows to balance cost, quality, compliance, and customer experience. Ultimately, the author advocates for a proactive stance on language risk, arguing that organizations can achieve greater compliance and customer satisfaction without incurring prohibitive costs—provided they adhere to best practices in language risk management.
