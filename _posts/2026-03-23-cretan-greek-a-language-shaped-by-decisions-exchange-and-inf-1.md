Language industry leaders will convene in Chania, Crete, this May for the ELIA Focus on Executives 2026, offering a unique opportunity to reflect on the rich linguistic heritage of the island. Cretan Greek, a regional dialect with a history shaped by various cultural influences, serves as a metaphor for the dynamic nature of language in today’s localization landscape. The evolution of Cretan Greek, influenced by Venetian, Byzantine, and Ottoman rule, mirrors the ongoing adaptation and negotiation that professionals in the language services industry face amid technological advancements and shifting communication patterns.

As organizations navigate the transformative impact of AI, the lessons from Cretan Greek remind us that language is never static; it evolves through interaction and context. This year's event will focus on how executives can create and defend value in an AI-driven industry, making it a crucial gathering for decision-makers.

For attendees, embracing local language nuances can enhance cultural engagement and foster connections, underscoring the importance of context in the localization process.
