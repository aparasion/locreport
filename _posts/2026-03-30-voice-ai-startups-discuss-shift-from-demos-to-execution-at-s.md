At SlatorCon Remote 2026, leaders from three language AI startups discussed a pivotal transition from experimental voice AI demos to fully integrated, production-ready systems. Panelists James Bolger (SimplyAI), Sam Adekunle (BimpeAI), and Michael Sujith (Wec.ai) emphasized the importance of embedding AI into existing business workflows, particularly in the telecom sector, where evolving customer expectations are outpacing AI deployment capabilities.

This shift is critical for the localization and language services industry, as it highlights the need for voice AI systems to support multilingual interactions natively rather than as an afterthought. Bolger noted that building trust with users hinges on the reliability of these systems, where accurate responses and seamless workflows are paramount. The discussion underscored that while creating voice AI prototypes is straightforward, scaling them for real-world applications requires significant investment in domain-specific tuning and governance.

For localization professionals, the key takeaway is the necessity of integrating multilingual capabilities from the outset to avoid undermining trust in AI interactions, ensuring that systems can effectively serve diverse markets.
