AI governance in healthcare is evolving, with a growing emphasis on communication quality alongside traditional concerns like algorithms and cybersecurity. A recent article highlights how language barriers can lead to imprecise data inputs in AI-generated documentation, particularly in embedded Electronic Health Records (EHR). This issue not only compromises data integrity but also reinforces inequities in patient care, as vital clinical details may be lost or inaccurately translated.

For localization and language services professionals, this shift underscores the critical need for integrating interpreter workflows into clinical systems. By ensuring consistent multilingual data structuring and monitoring communication gaps, organizations can enhance the reliability of AI inputs and improve overall patient outcomes. As healthcare leaders grapple with the rapid deployment of AI tools, addressing communication equity becomes essential to mitigate risks and foster trust in these technologies.
