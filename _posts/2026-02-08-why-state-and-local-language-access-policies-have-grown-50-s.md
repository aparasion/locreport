A significant shift in language access policies is underway, with a 50% increase since 2020, largely driven by the pandemic, demographic changes, and the need to address gaps left by federal mandates. Following the signing of Executive Order 14224 in 2025, which designated English as the official language and revoked previous federal language assistance requirements, many states and localities have taken the initiative to establish their own independent language service frameworks. 

This trend marks a pivotal change in how governments view multilingual service delivery, moving it from a secondary consideration to a core operational requirement. As of November 2025, 13 states and over 60 localities have adopted comprehensive language access policies, emphasizing infrastructure, staff training, and oversight to ensure effective communication with the 27.7 million Limited English Proficient individuals in the U.S.

For language service providers, this evolution presents both challenges and opportunities. Governments are seeking partners who can offer scalable, human-led services and training to meet these new standards.
