Translated has announced the upcoming release of its 2026 Research Report, “The Age of Experience in AI,” which heralds a transformative shift in artificial intelligence. This report, produced by Translated's research center Imminent, emphasizes the evolution from static, historical data-based models to AI systems that learn through real-time interactions with their environments. CEO Marco Trombetti highlights that this advancement will enable machines not only to process information but also to develop a form of sensory perception.

This shift is significant for the localization and language services industry, as it suggests a move towards more context-aware AI applications. The report identifies opportunities for new players and innovative design processes that can incorporate cultural plurality, ensuring AI systems are tailored to diverse linguistic and cultural contexts. The findings advocate for a decentralized approach to AI training, which could mitigate the risks of monopolization in intelligence.

For localization professionals, the key takeaway is the importance of integrating cultural sensitivity into AI development. As AI systems evolve, understanding and adapting to the nuances of different cultures will be crucial for creating effective and equitable language solutions.
