XTM International has unveiled a groundbreaking capability called Software Recorder, which allows enterprises to generate multilingual instructional videos at scale without the need for repetitive re-recording. By integrating its Rigi and Video Creation Cloud products, XTM positions itself as the only platform that seamlessly combines user interface (UI) capture, video creation, and translation into a single workflow. This innovation addresses a critical pain point for localization managers and language technology leaders: the inefficiency of creating and maintaining instructional videos that often become outdated with UI changes.

The launch of Software Recorder reflects a broader industry trend towards automation and efficiency in localization processes. As consumer preferences shift towards video content—evidenced by the Reuters Institute's finding that 65% of global consumers prefer video-based information—companies are under increasing pressure to produce high-quality, localized videos quickly. Traditional methods of video production often require extensive time and resources, particularly when multiple languages are involved. The urgency to adapt to these changing consumption habits is driving the need for solutions that not only streamline video creation but also ensure that content remains relevant and accurate across languages.

The introduction of Software Recorder significantly impacts localization workflows by eliminating the traditional re-record cycle. Localization teams can now capture a workflow once and automatically replicate it across various localized versions of the UI. This capability reduces the total cost of ownership for instructional videos by at least 60%, according to XTM. Roles such as localization managers and video production teams will find their workloads transformed, as they can focus on strategic initiatives rather than repetitive recording tasks. Additionally, the integration with translation management systems (TMS) ensures that terminology and tone remain consistent across all content, further enhancing the quality of localized materials.

The launch of Software Recorder signals a pivotal shift in the localization industry towards more integrated and automated solutions. As companies increasingly seek to manage global content across multiple languages and markets from a single platform, XTM’s approach exemplifies the potential for composable architecture in language services. This trend not only enhances operational efficiency but also aligns with the growing demand for agile content production in a fast-paced digital landscape. For localization professionals, embracing these innovations will be crucial in meeting the evolving needs of global audiences while maintaining high standards of quality and consistency.
