GLOBO emphasizes that empathy, rather than technical metrics, is the key to successful language access programs. In the language services industry, understanding the experiences of limited-English proficient (LEP) individuals is crucial, as their communication needs can significantly impact their healthcare, legal, and governmental interactions. GLOBO advocates for designing services that prioritize the needs of these individuals, recognizing that a poor experience can lead to serious consequences like missed diagnoses or misunderstood rights.

The company partners with organizations to develop tailored language access solutions that adapt to evolving needs. By investing in the support and training of interpreters and translators, GLOBO ensures that linguists are equipped to provide high-quality service, ultimately leading to better outcomes for LEP communities. This approach not only enhances the effectiveness of language services but also fosters trust and reflects the values of organizations committed to inclusivity.

For localization professionals, the takeaway is clear: embedding empathy into language access initiatives can transform service delivery and strengthen community relationships.
