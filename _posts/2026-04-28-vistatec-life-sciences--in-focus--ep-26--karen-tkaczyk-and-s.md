In a recent episode of the "Life Sciences – In Focus" podcast, Simon Hodgkins, CMO at Vistatec AI, emphasizes that while AI is making significant strides in life sciences, it serves as an augmentation tool rather than a replacement for human expertise. The discussion highlights the regulatory challenges and data privacy concerns that shape the application of AI in this highly regulated industry, where patient safety is paramount.

For localization professionals, the episode underscores the complexities of using AI for translation in clinical contexts. AI can generate multilingual content quickly, but it often falls short in cultural nuance, regulatory compliance, and contextual accuracy. The necessity of a “human-in-the-loop” approach, where multiple experts validate content, remains critical to ensure that patient-facing materials are culturally adapted and audit-ready.

The key takeaway is that while AI offers tremendous potential, its successful integration into life sciences requires careful consideration of regulatory frameworks and human oversight. This partnership between technology and expertise is essential for maintaining accuracy and trust in multilingual communication.
