DeepL is expanding its data infrastructure to enhance global performance by integrating AWS as a sub-processor, a move aimed at delivering low-latency, real-time language solutions to customers worldwide. This shift means DeepL will no longer process data exclusively within Europe, allowing for dynamic scaling of data hosting to meet increased demand across different time zones and markets.

For localization and language services professionals, this development highlights a significant trend towards global scalability in language technology. By leveraging AWS, DeepL can now support a broader customer base while maintaining stringent data privacy and security standards, including GDPR compliance and robust encryption protocols. The ability for customers to manage their own encryption keys ensures that they retain control over their data, even as infrastructure expands.

One key takeaway is that this expansion not only improves performance but also offers clients greater flexibility in meeting data residency requirements, making DeepL an increasingly viable option for enterprises with specific compliance needs.
