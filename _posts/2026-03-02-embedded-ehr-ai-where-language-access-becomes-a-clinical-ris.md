Language access is increasingly recognized as a critical "clinical risk variable" within AI-driven healthcare systems. As AI tools become integral to electronic health records (EHR), inaccuracies in language interpretation can lead to significant issues, including compromised patient safety and documentation errors. Executives in healthcare must address these risks to maintain trust in AI outputs and ensure effective patient care.

Ignoring language gaps in AI strategies can have far-reaching consequences, impacting clinical quality, coding accuracy, and clinician confidence. As AI tools draft clinical notes and inform decisions, the quality of the underlying communication is paramount. If language services are not embedded within EHR workflows, the risks of misinformation and inefficiencies multiply, ultimately affecting patient outcomes and organizational value.

To mitigate these risks, healthcare leaders should prioritize integrating communication integrity into their AI governance frameworks. This approach will ensure that multilingual encounters are accurately captured and utilized, fostering safer and more effective healthcare delivery.
