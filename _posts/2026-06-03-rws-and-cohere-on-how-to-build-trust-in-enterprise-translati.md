The landscape of enterprise translation is evolving rapidly, and the recent collaboration between RWS and Cohere highlights a pivotal shift towards more reliable and efficient language models. [slator.com](https://slator.com/rws-cohere-build-trust-enterprise-translation) reports that With the introduction of Language Weaver Pro, RWS aims to tackle the significant challenges posed by existing large language models (LLMs), particularly their tendency to produce fluent yet inaccurate translations—a phenomenon known as "hallucination." Ollie Stott, RWS's Language Weaver Product Manager, emphasizes that the focus should not merely be on the models themselves but on creating a new architecture tailored for enterprise needs. This approach is crucial for localization managers and enterprise language buyers who require not just speed but also accuracy in translation.

Cohere's research underscores a critical insight: a staggering 92% of public parallel data is deemed too simplistic for current models. This revelation suggests that many existing systems may not be leveraging the full potential of available data, leading to suboptimal translation quality. By refining their foundation model, Cohere claims that Language Weaver Pro can now match or exceed the performance of traditional references in this corpus. For localization managers, this means that adopting such advanced systems could significantly enhance the quality of translations, particularly in complex or nuanced content areas where precision is paramount.

The operational efficiency of Language Weaver Pro is another key selling point. With a target translation speed of one million words per minute and the ability to run on just two GPUs, the system is designed to meet the demands of high-volume translation environments. This capability is particularly appealing to language technology leaders who are often tasked with balancing performance and resource allocation. The ability to deploy this technology in private virtual clouds or fully air-gapped environments also addresses security concerns that many enterprises face, making it a versatile option for organizations with stringent data protection requirements.

As we look toward the future, the implications of this technological advancement are profound. The collaboration between RWS and Cohere not only promises to enhance the quality and speed of enterprise translations but also signals a broader trend towards more specialized and efficient language solutions. For localization managers and enterprise language buyers, embracing these innovations could lead to a significant competitive advantage in an increasingly globalized market. The evolution of translation technology is not just about keeping pace; it’s about redefining what’s possible in the realm of language services.
