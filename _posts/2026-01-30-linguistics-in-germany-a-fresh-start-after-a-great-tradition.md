This autobiographical article reflects on the life and scientific contributions of linguist Manfred Bierwisch, highlighting both his academic achievements and the challenging political context in which he operated. Compiled posthumously, the article integrates Bierwisch's own writings with excerpts from interviews and other texts to provide a comprehensive overview of his work. 

Key features of Bierwisch's research are discussed, including his innovative approaches to syntax and semantics, which have significantly influenced contemporary linguistic theory. The article also addresses the impact of the sociopolitical environment on his research trajectory, offering insights into how external factors shaped his scholarly pursuits.

The significance of this article lies not only in its tribute to Bierwisch's intellectual legacy but also in its exploration of the interplay between linguistics and political circumstances. For language science researchers, it underscores the importance of contextualizing linguistic work within broader historical and cultural frameworks, enriching our understanding of the discipline's evolution.
