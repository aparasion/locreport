Stanford University's Shorenstein Asia-Pacific Research Center (APARC) is a key institution focused on interdisciplinary research and policy dialogue concerning Asia's pressing challenges. The center aims to educate future Asia experts while fostering collaboration between research and practical applications through various publications and public engagement initiatives.

APARC provides critical, policy-relevant research to decision-makers in both the U.S. and Asia, enhancing the public's understanding of Asian affairs. Recent works by its scholars include titles addressing America's trade policy in the Asia-Pacific, China's rise as a global power, and the U.S. relationship with Southeast Asia.

Notably, former diplomat Rahm Emanuel recently participated in an APARC briefing, discussing the current political landscape in America and its implications for U.S. alliances in the Indo-Pacific. This dialogue underscores the center's commitment to addressing the intersection of domestic and international issues affecting the region.
