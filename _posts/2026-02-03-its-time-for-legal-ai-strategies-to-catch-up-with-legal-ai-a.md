The legal sector is experiencing a seismic shift in its approach to artificial intelligence (AI), with a recent DeepL survey revealing that 96% of legal professionals in the U.S. are now utilizing AI in their work. This rapid adoption, however, comes with a caveat: 71% of these professionals admit to using AI tools without formal approval, raising significant concerns about compliance, data security, and the quality of legal advice. As legal professionals, traditionally cautious about technology adoption, embrace AI at an unprecedented pace, it is crucial for localization managers and language technology leaders to understand the implications of this trend on their workflows and strategies.

This surge in AI adoption within the legal industry is part of a broader trend toward digital transformation across various sectors. Organizations are increasingly pressured to enhance efficiency and productivity, often at the expense of established protocols and compliance measures. In the legal field, this pressure is compounded by client demands for faster turnaround times and cost-effective solutions. The shift towards value-based billing models, as noted by a third of respondents in the survey, further underscores the urgency for legal teams to adapt quickly. As AI tools become integral to legal workflows, the need for a coherent strategy that aligns with compliance and quality standards has never been more pressing.

The implications for localization workflows are significant. Legal teams are now tasked with navigating a landscape where AI-generated content must be both accurate and compliant. This creates a demand for localization professionals who can ensure that legal documents, contracts, and communications are not only translated accurately but also adhere to the specific legal standards and cultural nuances of different jurisdictions. The rise of shadow AI usage indicates a gap in approved tools that meet these needs, leading to potential risks of data breaches and undermined credibility. Localization managers must therefore advocate for and implement AI solutions that prioritize security and compliance while enhancing the efficiency of legal translations.

This scenario signals a critical juncture for the localization industry. As legal professionals increasingly rely on AI, the demand for robust, compliant, and contextually aware translation tools will grow. Companies like DeepL, which are already addressing these needs by offering AI solutions tailored for the legal sector, will likely gain a competitive edge. The industry must recognize that the integration of AI should not merely be a reaction to external pressures but a strategic initiative that enhances the quality and reliability of legal services. Moving forward, localization leaders must champion the development of AI strategies that not only mitigate risks associated with shadow AI but also leverage AI's potential to transform legal workflows and elevate the overall quality of service delivery.
