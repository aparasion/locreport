The legal sector is experiencing a seismic shift in AI adoption, with a DeepL survey revealing that 96% of legal professionals now use AI in their work. This rapid integration is not just about efficiency; it's reshaping workflows and business models across firms. However, the survey also highlights a troubling trend: 71% of respondents admit to using AI tools without formal approval, raising concerns about compliance and data security in a field where these issues are paramount.

For localization and language services professionals, this trend underscores the urgent need for robust AI strategies that align with compliance and quality standards. As legal teams increasingly rely on AI for tasks like translation, the demand for secure, accurate tools that can handle the nuances of legal language is critical. This presents an opportunity for language technology providers to step in and offer solutions that not only enhance productivity but also ensure adherence to legal standards.

The key takeaway for the industry is the importance of proactive AI governance. Legal professionals need clear guidance on approved tools that meet security and compliance requirements, while language service providers must develop AI solutions that cater specifically to the legal context, ensuring both accuracy and regulatory adherence.
