Google is piloting a new "Speaking practice" feature in Search, aimed at enhancing conversational English skills for learners in select countries, including Argentina, India, and Mexico. This initiative, part of Google’s Search Labs, encourages users to engage in interactive language exercises powered by AI, allowing them to practice speaking in context and receive feedback on their grammar and clarity.

This development is significant for the localization and language services industry as it highlights the growing integration of AI in language learning. By enabling learners to participate in realistic conversational scenarios, Google is positioning itself as a potential competitor to established language learning platforms like Duolingo and Babbel. The feature builds upon previous tools aimed at improving pronunciation and grammar, indicating a shift toward more immersive and interactive learning experiences.

For localization professionals, the emergence of such AI-driven tools underscores the importance of adapting content and workflows to accommodate evolving language learning preferences, potentially reshaping how language services are delivered in the future.
