Unbabel, the Lisbon-based startup specializing in scalable customer service translation, has successfully raised $60 million in a Series C funding round led by Point72 Ventures. This latest investment brings Unbabel's total funding to $91 million since its inception in 2013. The company combines machine translation speed with the nuance of human expertise, leveraging a global community of over 100,000 freelance translators to enhance its platform.

This funding is significant for the localization and language services industry as it underscores the growing demand for efficient multilingual customer support solutions. Unbabel claims to offer cost savings of up to 76% compared to traditional translation methods, which is appealing for enterprises looking to optimize their customer engagement strategies. With an impressive client roster that includes major players like Facebook and Microsoft, Unbabel is poised to further refine its AI capabilities and expand its market presence.

Localization professionals should take note of Unbabel's approach to integrating AI with human translation, as it could set new standards for quality and efficiency in the industry.
