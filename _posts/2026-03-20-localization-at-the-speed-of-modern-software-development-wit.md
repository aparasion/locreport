Adam Asnes, CEO of Lingoport, recently shared insights on VistaTalks about the evolution of localization in software development. With a focus on automation and integration, Lingoport's approach emphasizes that localization must transition from a one-off task to a continuous, embedded process within the development lifecycle. This shift addresses the challenges faced by modern software teams, where rapid releases often leave localization as an afterthought, leading to inefficiencies and delays.

Asnes highlights the importance of integrating localization from the design phase, enabling teams to visualize how localized content fits within the software. By using tools like Lingoport Localizer, localization becomes a seamless part of the agile workflow, reducing bottlenecks and enhancing quality. The collaboration with Vistatec further enhances this process by applying AI-driven quality checks, ensuring that localization efforts are precise and contextually relevant.

For localization professionals, this discussion underscores the necessity of adapting to faster development cycles and embracing automation. I highly recommend exploring the full conversation to gain deeper insights into how these changes can benefit your localization strategy.
