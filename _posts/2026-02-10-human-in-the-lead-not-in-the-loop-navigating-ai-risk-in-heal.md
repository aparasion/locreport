At the recent Massachusetts Health & Hospital Association meeting, leaders emphasized the necessity of integrating health equity into system design rather than treating it as an afterthought. Keynote speaker Dr. Joseph Betancourt highlighted that language access for limited English proficient (LEP) patients is essential for equitable care, advocating for a "human-in-the-lead" approach to AI deployment in healthcare settings.

This perspective is crucial for the localization and language services industry, as it underscores the importance of embedding language support into every aspect of patient care. With the rise of AI and telehealth, ensuring that professional medical interpretation is prioritized can significantly enhance patient safety and outcomes, particularly for vulnerable populations facing barriers to access.

The key takeaway for localization professionals is the urgent need to advocate for systemic integration of language access in healthcare workflows. For a deeper understanding of these insights and their implications for the future of healthcare delivery, I recommend exploring the full article.
