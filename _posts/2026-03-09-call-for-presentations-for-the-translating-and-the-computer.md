The 47th Translating and the Computer Conference (TC47) is set to take place in Luxembourg from December 8 to 10, 2026, with a focus on the theme “AI-assisted or AI-eclipsed? Language Services between Promise and Pressure.” Organized by AsLing, the conference invites submissions from language professionals, researchers, and industry stakeholders to explore the transformative impact of AI on multilingual communication and the language services sector.

This conference is particularly relevant as it addresses pressing questions about the role of AI in language services, including its implications for human agency, ethical practices, and sustainable workflows. With the rapid evolution of machine translation and large language models, TC47 aims to foster discussions on how to ensure technology enhances rather than replaces human expertise, promoting inclusivity and trust in language services.

For professionals in localization and translation, TC47 presents a valuable opportunity to contribute to vital conversations on AI integration and its effects on the industry. I encourage you to check out the full details and consider submitting your insights to help shape the future of our field.
