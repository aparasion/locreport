Interactio, a remote interpretation platform that serves high-profile clients like the United Nations and Microsoft, has successfully raised $30 million in Series A funding. This significant investment comes on the heels of a 12x increase in platform usage during the pandemic, underscoring the growing demand for remote interpretation solutions as global meetings shift online. The funding, led by Eight Roads Ventures and Storm Ventures, will enable Interactio to enhance its technology and expand its client base, particularly among corporate clients.

As the localization and language services industry adapts to the rise of hybrid meetings, Interactio's focus on integrating with existing video conferencing tools like Zoom and Webex positions it as a key player. By offering a hybrid solution that combines remote and on-site interpretation, the company aims to meet the needs of clients requiring high-quality language services in complex meeting scenarios. 

For localization professionals, the takeaway is clear: as remote work becomes entrenched, the demand for reliable, scalable interpretation services will continue to grow, highlighting the importance of human interpreters in conveying nuanced communication that machines may miss.
