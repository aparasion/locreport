Porto, the host city for the ELIA Together 2026 conference, invites visitors to embrace its leisurely pace and rich culture. The city’s narrow streets and vibrant cafés create an atmosphere where enjoying life is paramount. While Portuguese is the primary language, many locals speak English, and using even a few Portuguese phrases can enhance interactions and foster connections.

Key phrases such as “olá” for greetings and “obrigado/obrigada” for thank you are encouraged, reflecting respect and friendliness. Coffee culture is significant in Porto, with various espresso styles to explore, and the famous “pastel de nata” is a must-try treat. As the day winds down, the golden evening light sets the stage for relaxed conversations over a glass of “vinho do Porto.”

Visitors are reminded that mastering the language is not necessary; simple phrases can make one feel at home in this charming city. For more insights and tips on navigating Porto, read the full article.
