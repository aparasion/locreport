At the recent Interpreter Summit in Leeds, freelance interpreter Shekoufeh Tahmasebi Nejad shared her extensive experience in interpreting Farsi and Dari, highlighting the critical role interpreters play in bridging communication gaps across cultures. With over twenty years in the field, she emphasized the importance of not just translating words but also conveying tone and emotion, particularly in sensitive legal contexts.

Her insights shed light on the evolving challenges interpreters face, including the complexities of remote interpreting and the need for continuous professional development. Nejad’s commitment to accuracy and empathy showcases how interpreters contribute to fair outcomes in legal settings, reinforcing the idea that effective communication can significantly impact individuals' lives.

For localization and language services professionals, Nejad’s experiences underline the necessity of understanding the nuanced demands of interpreting work.
