In a recent episode of VistaTalks, XTM CEO Lorcan Malone discussed the transformative state of the localization industry, emphasizing its evolution driven by AI, content volume, and the demand for speed. Malone, whose extensive career spans leadership roles across global tech companies, highlighted that localization is no longer a downstream function but is now embedded earlier in the content lifecycle, necessitating a shift in workflows.

Malone pointed out the growing tension between the increasing demands for rapid localization and stagnant budgets, which forces organizations to scale without additional resources while ensuring quality and compliance. He stressed that AI, while a powerful tool, is context-dependent and cannot replace human oversight for high-risk content. Instead, the future lies in harmonizing AI capabilities with human expertise, ensuring that workflows are intelligently orchestrated to maintain trust and quality.

For localization professionals, Malone’s insights underscore the importance of adaptability in today’s fast-paced environment. Embracing a flexible, ecosystem-driven approach that integrates AI and human skills will be crucial for organizations aiming to leverage localization as a core business capability and drive growth in global markets.
