Google has expanded its real-time translation capabilities to mobile devices, launching the feature on both Android and iOS globally. Users can activate the translation mode through Google Assistant by simply saying commands like, “Hey Google, be my German translator.” This functionality provides real-time transcripts and audio translations, enhancing communication across 44 languages—up from 29 previously available on smart speakers and displays.

This development is significant for the localization and language services industry as it showcases the increasing integration of AI-driven tools into everyday communication. The seamless incorporation of translation features into widely used applications like Google Assistant reduces the need for separate translation apps, streamlining workflows for users and potentially increasing demand for localized content in diverse languages.

Localization professionals should consider how this evolution in mobile translation technology could influence user expectations and the demand for high-quality, contextually relevant translations, particularly in travel and tourism sectors.
