Meta is expanding its AI-powered translation capabilities on Instagram and Facebook by adding support for Hindi and Portuguese, enhancing accessibility for users in key markets like India and Brazil. This move follows the initial rollout of the feature in August, which included English and Spanish, and aims to help creators reach wider audiences across linguistic and cultural barriers. Instagram head Adam Mosseri emphasized the potential for creators to grow their following by connecting with non-native speakers.

This development is significant for the localization and language services industry as it reflects a growing trend towards integrating AI-driven translation in social media platforms. As Meta enhances its translation features, including automatic dubbing and text translation for Reels, it highlights the demand for seamless multilingual content delivery. This shift could influence how businesses approach content creation and localization strategies.

Localization professionals should consider the implications of these advancements on their workflows, particularly in adapting content for diverse audiences and leveraging AI tools to enhance engagement in global markets.
