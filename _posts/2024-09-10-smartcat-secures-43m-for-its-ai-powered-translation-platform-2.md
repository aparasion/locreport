Smartcat, a leading provider of AI-driven translation tools, recently secured $43 million in Series C funding to enhance its offerings for enterprise clients. Founded in 2016, the company has quickly gained traction, serving over 1,000 corporate customers, including 20% of the Fortune 500. CEO Ivan Smolnikov emphasizes that while AI can deliver accurate translations, it often lacks the nuanced richness that human translators provide. Smartcat aims to bridge this gap by offering a combination of AI translation, human editing, and a matching engine that selects the best third-party models for specific content.

This development highlights a significant trend in the localization industry: the increasing reliance on AI technologies while still recognizing the indispensable value of human expertise. As enterprises seek scalable solutions for multilingual content, Smartcat's model offers a compelling alternative to traditional translation agencies, potentially reshaping workflows and market dynamics.

For localization professionals, the key takeaway is the importance of integrating AI with human oversight to enhance translation quality, ensuring that businesses can meet both accuracy and contextual richness in their multilingual communications.
