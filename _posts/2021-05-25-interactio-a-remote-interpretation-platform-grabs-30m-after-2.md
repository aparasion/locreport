Interactio, a remote interpretation platform serving high-profile clients like the United Nations and BMW, has secured $30 million in Series A funding, driven by a 12x increase in usage during the pandemic. The investment, led by Eight Roads Ventures and Storm Ventures, will enable Interactio to enhance its technology and user experience as it prepares for a surge in multilingual online meetings. The platform connects certified interpreters for real-time interpretation, supporting a hybrid model that integrates seamlessly with popular video conferencing tools.

This funding is significant for the localization and language services industry, highlighting the growing demand for professional interpretation in an increasingly digital world. Interactio's focus on customer support and its ability to scale interpreter resources positions it well to meet the needs of both institutional and corporate clients. As remote work becomes the norm, the company aims to expand its client base while maintaining high standards of interpretation quality.

A key takeaway for localization professionals is the importance of integrating technology with human expertise. While AI-driven translation tools are improving, the nuanced understanding provided by human interpreters remains crucial for effective communication in high-stakes environments.
