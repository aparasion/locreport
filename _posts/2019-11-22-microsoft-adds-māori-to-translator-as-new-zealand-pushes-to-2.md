Microsoft and the New Zealand government are collaborating to integrate the Māori language into Microsoft Translator, enhancing its accessibility and supporting efforts to revitalize te reo Māori. This initiative addresses the decline in Māori speakers, currently at just 3% of the population, by leveraging machine translation technology to make the language more relevant and widely used.

The partnership highlights the intersection of technology and cultural preservation, showcasing how AI-driven language tools can play a crucial role in supporting minority languages. By developing a robust translation model with the help of the Māori Language Commission, Microsoft aims to create a comprehensive corpus that ensures accurate translations, thereby facilitating broader engagement with Māori culture and education.

For localization professionals, this project underscores the importance of inclusive language technology in preserving cultural heritage. It serves as a reminder of the potential for machine translation to not only bridge communication gaps but also to support the survival of endangered languages in a globalized world.
