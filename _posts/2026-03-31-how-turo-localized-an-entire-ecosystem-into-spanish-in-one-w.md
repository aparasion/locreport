Turo, the innovative car rental marketplace, has rapidly scaled its operations to a $1 billion business by prioritizing localization as a key strategy for building trust with users. Under the leadership of Gideon Hod, Director of Product Operations, Turo has transitioned from a traditional localization model to an AI-driven workflow using Crowdin, enabling them to adapt their platform for diverse markets efficiently. This shift has allowed Turo to localize their entire ecosystem to Spanish in just one week, demonstrating the agility and cost-effectiveness of AI in localization.

This transformation is significant for the language services industry as it highlights a growing trend towards AI-driven workflows that can meet the fast-paced demands of global expansion. By moving away from traditional agency models, Turo has streamlined its localization process, reducing bottlenecks and enhancing responsiveness to user needs. The integration of AI tools not only accelerates translation but also maintains brand voice consistency across languages.

For localization professionals, Turo's experience underscores the importance of leveraging AI to enhance operational efficiency and adapt to market dynamics swiftly. As companies like Turo embrace these technologies, it signals a shift in how localization will be approached, emphasizing speed, scalability, and user-centric strategies.
